The Basics

What is Policy Debate?

Policy debate (also called Cross-Examination Debate or CX) is a two-on-two competitive debate format where teams argue for and against a government policy resolution across an academic year.

🏛

The Resolution

Each year, a national resolution proposes a specific policy change. Teams debate the same topic all season, building deep expertise.

🤝

Teams of Two

An Affirmative team defends the resolution; a Negative team opposes it. Each debater gives multiple speeches and cross-examinations.

⏱

Speech Times

Rounds include 1AC, 1NC, 2AC, 2NC, 1NR, 1AR, 2NR, and 2AR speeches — typically 8 minutes each with 3-minute cross-ex periods.

📚

Research Heavy

Policy debate rewards thorough research. Teams compile evidence "blocks" from academic sources to support their arguments.
